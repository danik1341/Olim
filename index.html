Education

Schooling Options for Children

If you're moving to Israel with children, you'll need to explore the schooling options available. Here are some key considerations:

  • Public education in Israel is free and mandatory for children aged 3 to 18.
  • Public schools (mamlachti) follow the national curriculum and are available in most communities.
  • Private schools (mamlachti dati or mamlachti charedi) offer religious education alongside the standard curriculum.
  • International schools provide education in various languages and cater to expatriate communities.
  • Consider the location and quality of schools when choosing a neighborhood or city to reside in.
  • Research and visit schools to assess their facilities, teaching methods, and extracurricular activities.

Hebrew Language Learning Resources

Learning Hebrew is essential for integration and communication in Israel. Here are some resources to help you learn the language:

  • Ulpan classes are Hebrew language courses offered throughout Israel. They cater to various proficiency levels and can provide a structured learning experience.
  • Online platforms like Duolingo, Memrise, and Babbel offer Hebrew language courses that you can access anytime and anywhere.
  • Language exchange programs and language learning apps, such as Tandem, can connect you with native Hebrew speakers for practice and conversation.
  • Israeli television shows, movies, and music can be excellent resources for immersing yourself in the language and improving your listening skills.
  • Consider joining local Hebrew conversation groups or language exchange meetups to practice speaking with native speakers.

Higher Education Institutions

Israel is renowned for its higher education institutions, offering a wide range of academic programs. Here are some notable universities and colleges:

  • The Hebrew University of Jerusalem is one of Israel's oldest and most prestigious universities, offering a variety of undergraduate and graduate programs.
  • Technion - Israel Institute of Technology is a leading institution for science, engineering, and technology studies.
  • Tel Aviv University is known for its diverse academic offerings, including social sciences, humanities, business, and the arts.
  • Ben-Gurion University of the Negev is recognized for its focus on desert research, environmental studies, and engineering.
  • Weizmann Institute of Science specializes in scientific research and offers advanced programs in mathematics, physics, and biology.
  • Consider researching specific departments, programs, and admission requirements to find the best fit for your academic goals.

Employment

Job Search Tips and Websites -

When searching for a job in Israel, it's important to utilize various resources and strategies to increase your chances of success. Here are some tips and websites to consider:

  • Visit online job portals such as Jobnet and AllJobs to browse available job listings.
  • Create a professional profile on LinkedIn and connect with professionals in your field. LinkedIn is widely used for networking and job searching.
  • Consider reaching out to recruitment agencies and job placement services that specialize in your industry or profession.
  • Networking is crucial in Israel. Attend industry events, join professional associations, and engage in networking activities to expand your contacts and opportunities.
  • Keep your CV (resume) updated and tailored to the Israeli job market. Highlight relevant skills, experience, and achievements.
  • Consider learning Hebrew, as it can significantly enhance your employment prospects, especially in certain industries.

Networking and Professional Associations -

Networking is highly valued in Israeli work culture. Building professional connections and engaging with industry-specific associations can provide valuable insights and opportunities. Here are some suggestions:

  • Join professional associations and organizations related to your field of interest. These associations often organize events, seminars, and workshops that can facilitate networking.
  • Attend industry conferences, trade shows, and exhibitions to connect with professionals, learn about trends, and showcase your expertise.
  • Utilize online platforms such as LinkedIn and industry-specific forums to connect with professionals, join relevant groups, and participate in discussions.
  • Consider seeking mentors or advisors who can guide you in your career journey and provide valuable industry insights.
  • Attend networking events and social gatherings, such as meetups or industry-specific events, to expand your professional network.

Understanding Israeli Work Culture -

Familiarizing yourself with Israeli work culture can greatly contribute to your professional success. Here are some key aspects to consider:

  • Israeli work culture tends to be fast-paced and dynamic, with an emphasis on efficiency and results.
  • Direct communication is valued, and Israelis tend to be straightforward and assertive in expressing their opinions.
  • Building personal connections and networking play a crucial role in professional relationships and opportunities.
  • Punctuality is important, so strive to arrive on time for meetings and appointments.
  • Flexibility and adaptability are highly regarded, as Israeli companies often operate in a rapidly changing environment.
  • Take the time to learn Hebrew, as it can improve your integration into the workplace and enhance communication.

Healthcare

Public and Private Healthcare Providers

In Israel, you have access to both public and private healthcare providers. Public healthcare providers include government-owned hospitals, clinics, and medical centers. Some well-known public hospitals include Sheba Medical Center, Ichilov Hospital, and Hadassah Medical Center. Private healthcare providers offer additional options and services, usually with shorter wait times and more personalized care. Some private hospitals and clinics include Assuta Medical Centers, Herzliya Medical Center, and Tel Aviv Sourasky Medical Center (private wing).

Health Insurance Options

Health insurance in Israel is mandatory for all residents. There are several health insurance options available, including both public and private plans. The public health insurance system is called Bituach Leumi, and it provides basic coverage for all Israeli citizens and residents. Additionally, private health insurance plans are available to supplement the public coverage and offer extended services and benefits. Some popular private health insurance providers in Israel include Clalit, Maccabi, Meuhedet, and Leumit.

Finding Doctors and Specialists

Finding doctors and specialists in Israel is relatively easy. You can start by consulting with your health insurance provider to get a list of approved doctors and specialists. They can recommend healthcare professionals based on your specific needs. You can also use online directories and platforms, such as the Ministry of Health's website, to search for doctors and specialists in your area. Additionally, word-of-mouth recommendations from friends, colleagues, or local communities can be valuable in finding trusted healthcare professionals.
