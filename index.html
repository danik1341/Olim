Before/After Arrival

Before Arrival

  1. Begin planning for Alyah, both financially and personally.
  2. Enhance your professional preparation, preferably arriving with a diploma to be validated.
  3. Consult the Jewish agency or global center for a list of rights, including the benefits you will receive.
  4. Inquire about the region you intend to settle in and ask about absorption centers.
  5. After receiving authorization and/or a stamp in your passport, try to resolve any remaining issues in your home country to avoid having to return soon, unless you have the financial means to do so.
  6. Try to learn Hebrew at least to a basic level before leaving your home country.
  7. For children or individuals with disabilities, specifically inquire about their rights before leaving your home country.
  8. Familiarize yourself, if possible, with Bituach Leumi (National Insurance) and Kupat Cholim (Healthcare Fund).

Upon arriving in Israel

  1. Visit the Misrad Hapnim (Ministry of Interior) to obtain a Teudat Zehut (identity card).
  2. Open a bank account as soon as possible.
  3. Always carry your Teudat Oleh, Teudat Zehut, and Cefach.
  4. Seek out an Ulpan Ivrit (Hebrew language course) as soon as possible. Information is available through non-governmental organizations and public/private Ulpanim.
  5. Enroll your children in a school (gather information about schools).
  6. Inquire with your bank if their application works in a language other than Hebrew and ask for assistance.
  7. Demand that the treatment of Olim (immigrants) be respectful and free from prejudice.

Phone and Internet

Mobile Phone Operators and Plans

    Cellcom -

  • Package: Cellcom offers various packages suitable for different needs, including voice, data, and international calling options.
  • Pricing: Prices start at 100 NIS per month for a basic package, with additional features and data options available at higher price points.

  • Package: Partner offers a range of voice and data packages tailored to different user preferences, including unlimited calling plans and data add-ons.
  • Pricing: Prices start at 80 NIS per month for a basic package, with prices varying based on the selected plan and additional features.

Internet Service Providers

    Bezeq -

  • Package: Bezeq provides various internet packages with different speeds and data allowances, including both wired and wireless connections.
  • Pricing: Prices start at 100 NIS per month for a basic internet plan, with higher speeds and unlimited data options available at higher price points.

    • HOT -

  • Package: HOT offers high-speed internet packages suitable for both residential and business users, with additional services such as Wi-Fi and TV bundles.
  • Pricing: Prices start at 120 NIS per month for a basic internet plan, with pricing varying based on the selected speed and additional services.

    • Partner -

  • Package: Partner provides internet services with different speed options and bundles, including combined packages with TV and phone services.
  • Pricing: Prices start at 110 NIS per month for a basic internet plan, with prices varying based on the selected speed and bundle options.

Comparing Packages and Prices

It is recommended to compare the packages and prices offered by different phone companies and internet service providers to find the best option that suits your needs. Prices and packages may vary over time, so it's advisable to visit the respective companies' websites or contact their customer service for up-to-date information on current packages, pricing, and any special offers.

Finances

  1. Seek assistance in a language you understand: When dealing with banks or financial institutions in Israel, try to find representatives who can communicate with you in a language you are comfortable with. English is commonly spoken, but services may also be available in Russian, French, Spanish.
  2. Check the language availability of bank apps: Before choosing a bank, inquire whether their mobile application is available in a language you understand. This will facilitate your financial transactions and make it easier to manage your accounts.
  3. Adjust to the high cost of living: Keep in mind that Israel has a relatively high cost of living, especially when it comes to food and housing. Be prepared for these expenses and plan your budget accordingly.
  4. Language preferences for bank SMS: Check with your bank to ensure that you can receive SMS notifications in a language you understand. This will help you stay informed about your account activity and transactions.
  5. Understand documents in Hebrew: When signing any documents in Hebrew, don't hesitate to ask for a detailed explanation of their contents. Make sure you fully comprehend the terms and conditions before signing, and don't let any language barrier prevent you from understanding the agreements you are entering into.
  6. Utilize multilingual ATMs and machines: Most ATMs and similar machines in Israel, such as Rav-Kav machines for public transportation, offer language options including Hebrew, Russian, French, and English. Take advantage of these options to conduct transactions conveniently and comfortably.
  7. Be cautious about freezing of accounts: It is important to note that both the government and banks in Israel may freeze accounts, even when there is no wrongdoing involved. This is especially true if you receive money from abroad that originates from an account not owned by the same person as the receiving account. Therefore, it is advisable to spread your funds across multiple accounts and exercise caution to avoid any inconvenience.
